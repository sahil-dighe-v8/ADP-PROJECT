What is Computer Vision?

Computer Vision (CV) is a field of Artificial Intelligence that enables machines to see, interpret, and make decisions based on visual data.

Key Applications

  • Face recognition in security and smartphones
  • Autonomous vehicles (self-driving cars) for navigation and obstacle detection
  • Medical imaging analysis for faster and accurate diagnosis
  • Object detection and classification in industrial automation
  • Surveillance systems for anomaly detection
  • Retail analytics (e.g., counting customers, tracking products)

Working Steps

  • Image Acquisition: Capturing images from cameras or sensors
  • Preprocessing: Noise reduction, resizing, and normalization
  • Feature Extraction: Detecting important patterns or edges
  • Segmentation: Dividing image into meaningful regions
  • Recognition & Classification: Identifying objects or patterns
  • Decision Making: Using predictions for actions or insights

Benefits

  • Automation of visual tasks
  • Improved accuracy over human observation
  • Faster data processing
  • Applications in healthcare, security, robotics, and more

Challenges

  • Privacy concerns with surveillance
  • Bias in training datasets
  • Complexity in understanding real-world environments
  • High computational resources required for deep learning models
